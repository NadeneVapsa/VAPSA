VAPSA Brand Guidelines

Veteran Advocacy Professional Standards Association

Introduction

This document outlines the brand guidelines for the Veteran Advocacy Professional Standards Association (VAPSA). It includes the vision, mission, strategic priorities, and design standards to ensure consistent and professional representation of the brand.

Vision

A fair, consistent, and compassionate compensation system that honours the service and sacrifice of every Australian veteran.

Mission

To lead systemic reform, national advocacy and the professional representation of veterans seeking compensation. We support veterans and their families by setting the highest standards in training, accreditation and advocacy, while promoting accountability from government and across the sector.

Strategic Priorities & Goals

1. Veteran-Centred Representation

Goal: Deliver and support expert, trauma-informed representation for veterans in compensation claims, reviews, and appeals.

  • Establish a National Register of accredited veteran compensation advocates.
  • Provide direct and referred representation services where needed.
  • Support complex, systemic, and test-case advocacy that sets precedents for improved access.
  • Prioritise outreach to under-served veteran communities (First Nations, women, younger veterans, remote and regional areas).

2. Accreditation, Training & Standards

Goal: Build a nationally consistent framework for the training, accreditation, and ongoing professional development of advocates.

  • Design and deliver a national certification program for veterans’ compensation advocates.
  • Develop core competencies, ethical standards, and CPD requirements for accredited representatives.
  • Support a mentoring and supervision model to ensure the delivery of safe, high-quality advice.
  • Partner with legal, academic, and veteran service bodies to ensure evidence-based learning content.

3. Policy Leadership & Reform

Goal: Influence the reform of the veterans’ compensation framework to ensure it is streamlined, equitable, and veteran-focused.

  • Lead national consultations with advocates and veteran communities to develop reform priorities.
  • Provide expert submissions and public commentary on legislation and policy affecting veterans’ entitlements.
  • Monitor and report on systemic delays, inconsistencies, and trends in case law across compensation systems.
  • Advocate for legislative simplification across MRCA, DRCA, and VEA.

4. Sector Coordination & Support

Goal: Strengthen the capability and collaboration of organisations and advocates working in the veterans’ compensation space.

  • Act as the peak representative body for compensation-focused organisations and advocates.
  • Host national forums, roundtables, and working groups to build consensus and sector alignment.
  • Provide tools, templates, policy updates, and legal briefings to support frontline advocacy among members.
  • Support workforce sustainability, succession planning, and mental health in the advocacy sector.

5. Public Awareness & System Accountability

Goal: Build public and political understanding of veterans' compensation issues and drive accountability across the system.

  • Run targeted media and public campaigns highlighting the experiences of veterans and advocates.
  • Report regularly on compensation system performance using data and case studies outcomes.
  • Foster relationships with key departments (e.g. DVA, Attorney-General’s Dept., Parliament) to address emerging issues.
  • Uphold the rights of veterans through systemic advocacy, legal partnerships, and ombudsman engagement.

Primary Logo

  • The VAPSA logo features the acronym in bold uppercase, with the full name displayed below. A circular emblem with arrows in blue and green symbolises progress, unity, and direction.
  • Clear Space: Maintain a clear space equal to the height of the 'V'—minimum Size: 100px (digital), 25mm (print).
  • Incorrect Usage: Do not stretch, distort, recolour, or place on low-contrast backgrounds.

Design Elements

Use clean, minimal layouts with ample white space. Incorporate brand colours and fonts consistently. Icons and illustrations should be flat and match the brand palette.

Graphic Elements

Use imagery that reflects veteran support, professionalism, and a sense of community. Prefer natural lighting and authentic moments. Ensure diversity and inclusivity in visuals.

Brand Voice and Messaging

  • Voice: Professional, supportive, and authoritative.
  • Tone: Empathetic, clear, and empowering.
  • Messaging should reflect VAPSA’s commitment to advocacy, integrity, and reform.
